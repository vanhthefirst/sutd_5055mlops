Campus Life

Discover more to life at SUTD than academics! Explore our campus designed for interaction and collaboration, and join our supportive community of fellow students, staff, and faculty.
Student experience

A deep dive into our student life

Find out what makes university life vibrant—from student organisations and co-curricular activities, to student awards and part-time work opportunities—as you balance academics, passion and leisure.

Global experience and exchange

The world is a classroom, and we offer various overseas opportunities for our students to embark on new adventures. We also welcome students from our partner institutions to SUTD and Singapore.

Career development

At SUTD, we maintain a robust network connecting our students, potential employers, and alumni through various platforms and programmes. We believe in starting career preparation early, by offering workshops and internships to help our students succeed.

Undergraduate Opportunities Programme (UxOP)

At SUTD, we offer programmes that take our students beyond the classroom, providing them with opportunities to gain new, valuable experiences and skills.
Undergraduate Teaching Opportunities Programme (UTOP)
UTOP gives students opportunities to impart and share, with fellow peers, the knowledge they’ve learned over their course of study in SUTD.
Undergraduate Research Opportunities Programme (UROP)
UROP inspires greater learning and curiosity among our undergraduates, by engaging faculty and students with innovative, cutting-edge research projects. At SUTD, you can start working on research projects right from day one.
Undergraduate Practice Opportunities Programme (UPOP)
UPOP prepares our undergraduates to be career-ready, through practical and hands-on sessions held by career advisors and professional consultants.

Freshmore (Terms 1 – 2)
Integrated into the Freshmore experience, residential living complements cohort-based learning, fostering community, ownership, and lasting friendships.
Undergraduates
Undergraduates looking to stay at the University’s student housing blocks after Term 2 should submit their applications online, during the main housing application exercise held each February.
Graduates
Graduate students and students moving on to Term 9 who wish to stay at the University’s student housing blocks should submit an online application during the housing application exercise, held each July. An additional housing exercise may be held in November for students who wish to stay from the January term, subject to vacancies.
Non-graduating students
Incoming exchange students are welcome to apply for student housing during designated housing application exercises. Applications may be considered on a first-come, first-served basis.
Staff
Staff housing options range from one- to four-bedroom apartments, to single rooms within a shared apartment—all within walking distance to the Sports and Recreation Centre, as well as our academic and administrative buildings.
Visitors
SUTD offers housing to visitors on official business at the University. Visitors who are keen to stay on campus are advised to submit their housing application at least two weeks prior to their arrival. Vacancies are subject to availability.

Wellbeing services

Emotional and mental health is the key to uncovering the best possible version of yourself. We aim to provide services and resources that help our students prioritise their wellbeing, and get the support they need.
Student counselling
We offer free, professional counselling for students—along with talks, workshops, and training to promote mental health awareness among our community.
Self-help resources
Get access free self-help and psychoeducational material, or discover other community resources available.
Protocol
Our Wellbeing Services abide by professional counselling codes of conduct and ethics, to ensure a safe and secure environment for your needs. Find out more about our guidelines and parameters that guide our counsellors' approach.

Please note that SUTD’s counselling services are not available to alumni and members of the general public. Check out our FAQs to find out more.
